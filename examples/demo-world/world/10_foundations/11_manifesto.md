# 11 Manifesto

## The Core Friction

Harbor Line treats the port as memory infrastructure: cargo moves, but what stays is the paperwork, the rust, and the stories clerks repeat at shift change. We document freight folklore against systems that want every box to look identical.

## The Core Emotion

Belonging-without-sentiment.

## The Founding Myth

In 1974 a night dispatcher at Pier 9 logged a shipment that never cleared customs yet appeared on three manifests. The duplicate entries became a zine, then a radio show, then a label for objects that "arrived twice."

## Non-Negotiable Beliefs

1. Paper evidence outranks oral legend unless both exist, then show both.
2. Industrial surfaces stay honest: no faux patina spray, no distressed filters in-camera.
3. Rectangles belong to institutions; rounded forms belong to care objects and food.
4. Color signals danger before beauty; accent red is for warnings, not decoration.
5. Public copy never promises arrival times the world cannot keep.

## The Enemy

Myth-washing logistics brands, glossy maritime nostalgia, and UI patterns that hide latency behind skeleton loaders.

## Physical Constraint (optional bridge)

All primary packaging uses uncoated 80 lb text, black ink only, with one Pantone spot per run.
